## Types of Questions
in IELTS Speaking
Part 1 Types
Part 1 is simple, daily, factual & opinion-based — short answers only.
Main question types:
1. Yes / No & Preference questions- Do you like…?
- Do you often…?
- Do you prefer A or B?
2. Wh- questions (what, where, when, why, how)- What do you usually do…?
- When do you…?
- Why do you like…?
- How often do you…?
3. Comparison questions- Is it different from…?
- Do people in your country usually…?
- How has it changed?
4. General & personal facts- Where are you from?
- Are you working or studying?
- What’s your hometown like?

Part 2 Types
Part 2 is a long monologue (1–2 mins) — you describe one topic in detail.
Main types of cue card topics:
1. People- Describe a person you admire / a friend / a family member / a famous person.
2. Places- Describe a city / a quiet place / a natural place / a place you visited.
3. Objects / Things- Describe a gift / a photo / a book / a gadget / something you made.
4. Events & Experiences- Describe a happy event / a trip / a party / a competition / a difficult situation.
5. Skills & Qualities- Describe a skill you learned / a good decision / a hobby / a useful thing.
6. Media & Entertainment- Describe a film / a song / a TV program / a website.
Each cue card always has 4 sub-questions:
- What it is
- When/where you saw/had it
- Who you were with / what happened
- How you felt / why you liked it

IELTS Speaking Structure (Simple & Easy to Memorize)
Part 1 Structure
Rule: Short, clear answers — 2–3 sentences each.
Basic Formula
1. Direct answer (Yes / No / I like… / I usually…)
2. Reason / example (Because… / For example…)
3. Extra small detail (optional, 1 phrase)
Example
Q: Do you like reading?
A: Yes, I do.
I enjoy reading novels because it helps me relax.
I usually read before going to bed.


Part 2 Structure (Cue Card)
Speak 1–2 minutes, follow this 4-part structure exactly.
1. Introduction (1 sentence)
- Directly say what you’re going to talk about.- Today I’d like to talk about …
2. Basic facts (answer the first 2 bullet points)
- What it is
- When / where / who with- It happened … / I went there … / It is …
3. Details & story (the main part)
- Describe what happened / how it was
- Add feelings, actions, small stories- First … Then … After that …
4. Conclusion (how you felt & why it matters)
- Explain why it was special / important- I really enjoyed it because … / It was memorable for me.
Simple Template You Can Memorize
Part 1
Yes/No + Because + Example
Part 2
1. I’d like to talk about ________.
2. It is ________. I experienced it ________.
3. What happened was ________.
4. I felt ________ because ________.

## Types of Questions in Part 3

IELTS Speaking Part 3
1. Types of Questions in Part 3
Part 3 is abstract, general, social, comparative — not about you personally anymore.
Common types:
1. Discuss / Opinion- What do you think about…? - How do people feel about…?
2. Advantages & Disadvantages- What are the benefits of…? - Are there any drawbacks?
3. Comparison (past vs now / young vs old / men vs women / city vs country)- How is X different from the past? - Do young people do the same as older people?
4. Cause & Effect (Why…?)- Why do people…? - What causes…?
5. Solutions / Suggestions- How can we solve this problem? - What should governments do?
6. Future prediction- How will X change in the future? - Do you think X will become more common?
7. Impact / Influence- How does X affect people’s lives?


2. Answer Structures for Part 3

Use these fixed templates — they work for every question type.

- A. Opinion Question
	- Structure: 1. Direct opinion 2. Reason 3. Example / further explanation
	- Template: - Personally, I think that… - The main reason is… - For instance,…
- B. Advantage / Disadvantage
	- Structure: 1. Topic sentence 2. 1–2 points 3. Explain each
	- Template: - Well, there are several clear benefits. - Firstly,… - In addition,… - However, there are also some downsides, such as…
- C. Comparison (past / now / young / old)
	- Structure: 1. General difference 2. One side 3. The other side 4. Maybe reason
	- Template: - I think there’s a big difference between… - In the past, people used to… - But nowadays, they tend to… - This is probably because…
- D. Why…? (Cause & Effect)
	- Structure: 1. Answer the “why” 2. 1–2 causes 3. Result / example
	- Template: - I suppose the main reason is… - Another factor could be… - As a result,…
- E. Solution Question
	- Structure: 1. General statement 2. Individual solution 3. Government / society solution 4. Result
	- Template: - There are a few possible ways to deal with this. - For individuals, they could… - Also, the government might… - If this happens,…
- F. Future Question
	- Structure: 1. Prediction 2. Reason 3. Possible result
	- Template: - I believe that… will probably… in the future. - This is because… - So it’s likely that…


Super Short Version (Easy to Memorize)
- Opinion: Opinion → Reason → Example
- Advantages: Firstly… Also… However…
- Compare: Past/Old → Now/Young → Because
- Why: Main reason → Another reason → Result
- Solutions: Individuals → Government → Effect
- Future: Will… because… so…
