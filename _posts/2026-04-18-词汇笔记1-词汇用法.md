## Parts of speech 词性

### Noun 名词

 

A noun names person, place, thing, animal, quality, action, idea, feeling, state.

Classification By Meaning
1. Common Noun 普通名词: General name, no capital letter: boy, city, coffee, water, teacher, book
2. Proper Noun 专有名词: Special name, capital first letter: Lucy, China, Monday, January, Starbucks, the Yellow River
3. Abstract Noun 抽象名词: No shape, can’t touch/see: happiness, sadness, health, love, time, sleep, freedom
4. Collective Noun 集合名词: Name a group of people/things: family, class, team, crowd, government, audience



- Countable/Uncountable 1. Countable Noun 可数名词: Can count, has singular & plural: cup → cups, student → students, apple → apples 2. Uncountable Noun 不可数名称: Can’t count directly, no plural form: coffee, water, milk, rice, money, weather
- Functions of a Noun in a Sentence： Subject - Object - Predicative - Object of preposition - Appositive
- Example: Coffee tastes good. → Coffee = Subject I like coffee. → coffee = Object
- Common Mistake： Don’t add “s” to uncountable nouns: ❌ coffees (wrong when meaning drink) ✅ a cup of coffee

 

### Pronoun 代词

A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repeating the same word again and again.



Classification
1. Personal Pronoun 人称代词： Subject form: I, you, he, she, it, we, they。 Object form: me, you, him, her, it, us, them
2. Possessive Pronoun 物主代词： Adjective form: my, your, his, her, its, our, their。 Noun form: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
3. Reflexive Pronoun 反身代词： myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves
4. Demonstrative Pronoun 指示代词： this, that, these, those
5. Interrogative Pronoun 疑问代词： who, whom, whose, what, which
6. Indefinite Pronoun 不定代词： someone, anyone, everyone, something, nothing, all, many, few



Functions：Same as nouns: can be subject, object, predicative.

Example: She likes coffee. (Subject) I gave him a cup. (Object)

 

### Verb 动词

A verb expresses:

- Action (do something)
- State / Being (exist / be something)

Classification By Meaning
1. Action Verb 实义动词

   - Transitive Verb 及物：need an object： I drink coffee.

   - Intransitive Verb 不及物：no object： He smiles.


2. Linking Verb 系动词 Connect subject and description: am, is, are, was, were， become, get, turn, seem, look, sound, taste Example: Coffee tastes nice.

3. Auxiliary Verb 助动词 Help main verb make tense, question, negative: do/does/did, have/has, will, can, may, must Example: I can drink coffee.



Classification By Tense Form

- Regular verbs: work → worked
- Irregular verbs: drink → drank → drunk



Core Rule：Every complete sentence must have a verb.No verb = no sentence.

 

### Adjective 形容词

An adjective modifies / describes nouns and pronouns only.

It answers: What kind? / Which one? / How many? / How much?



Classification
1. Descriptive Adjective 性质形容词 hot, cold, sweet, beautiful, busy
2. Quantitative Adjective 数量形容词 one, two, some, many, few
3. Demonstrative Adjective 指示形容词 this cup, that shop, these people



Position Rule
1. Usually before noun: hot coffee
2. After linking verb: The coffee is hot.



Degrees of Adjectives：Positive → Comparative → Superlative：hot → hotter → hottest，good → better → best。



Key Rule：Adjective never modifies verb. That’s adverb’s job.

 

### Adverb 副词

Modifies: Verb / Adjective / Another Adverb / Whole Sentence
Answers: How? When? Where? How often? How much?



Main Types
1. Manner: slowly, happily, clearly
2. Time: now, then, today, soon
3. Place: here, there, outside
4. Frequency: always, usually, often, sometimes, never
5. Degree: very, quite, too, really, almost



Critical Difference: Adjective vs Adverb

- Adjective → only describe noun
- Adverb → describe verb / adj / adverb

Example: He is a quick boy. (adj + noun) He runs quickly. (adv + verb)



Position Rule。Adverbs can be:

- At the beginning
- In the middle
- At the end of a sentence

 

副词（adv.） 用来修饰动词、形容词、其他副词、整句话。
说明时间、地点、程度、方式、频率、疑问等。
简单记：修饰名词用形容词，修饰别的都常用副词。

副词分类 + 用法例句

- 方式副词（怎么做） 修饰动词，表动作方式 常见：quickly, slowly, carefully, happily, loudly - He runs **fast**. 他跑得快。 - She speaks English **well**. 她英语说得好。
- 时间副词（什么时候） now, then, today, yesterday, soon, already 位置：句首/句尾 - I will go **tomorrow**. - **Now** let’s start.
- 地点副词（在哪里） here, there, home, abroad, upstairs - Come **here**. - He went **abroad**.
- 频率副词（多久一次） always > usually > often > sometimes > seldom > never **位置：be动词后，实义动词前** - He **always** gets up early. - She is **never** late.
- 程度副词（到什么程度） very, quite, rather, too, really, almost
  - **修饰形容词/副词** - It’s **very** cold. - He runs **quite** fast.
  - 修饰动词 动词前  They **really** enjoy the movie. almost, nearly, hardly, barely, really, a little, a bit, completely, totally。放句尾 much /very much /a lot 非常（修饰动词）
- 疑问副词（提问） when 何时、where 何地、why 为什么、how 怎样 - **Where** do you live? - **Why** are you late?

副词位置规则：句首、句中、句尾

1. **修饰动词**：一般放**句尾** She sings **beautifully**.
2. **修饰形容词/副词**：放**前面** **very** good / **too** quickly
3. **频率副词**： ✅ be动词后：He **is** often busy. ✅ 实义动词前：He **often** goes there.
4. **多个副词顺序**：方式 → 地点 → 时间：   He runs **fast**（方式） **in the park**（地点） **every morning**（时间）.



形容词变副词规律

1. 一般直接 + **ly**： quick → quickly

2. 以 **y** 结尾：变 y 为 i + ly： happy → happily, easy → easily

3. 不规则变化（必背） good → **well** fast → **fast**（形副同形） hard → hard 努力地；hardly 几乎不
- 易混点 - **hard** 努力地 / **hardly** 几乎不 - **late** 迟到 / **lately** 最近 - **near** 在附近 / **nearly** 几乎，fast → **fast**（形副同形），friend->friendly
- 形容词修饰名词，对应的副词修饰动词



### Preposition 介词

Shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and other words in time, place, direction, possession.

Common Prepositions

- Time: in, on, at, before, after, during
- Place: in, on, under, beside, near, behind
- Others: with, for, to, from, by, about



Golden Rule

1. A preposition must have an object (noun/pronoun) after it.
2. Preposition never stands alone.
3. After preposition, use object pronoun: ✅ with me / with him ❌ with I / with he



Example:
I drink coffee in the morning.
in = preposition
the morning = object of preposition

 

### Conjunction 连词



Connects words, phrases, clauses, sentences.



3 Major Types
1. Coordinating Conjunction 并列连词 FANBOYS: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so Connect equal parts.
2. Subordinating Conjunction 从属连词 because, if, when, although, while, since Connect main clause + subordinate clause.
3. Correlative Conjunction 关联连词 both…and, either…or, neither…nor, not only…but also



Example: I love coffee and tea. I drink coffee because I like it.

 

### Interjection 感叹词

Express sudden strong feeling: surprise, joy, pain, anger, excitement.

Features
1. Has no grammatical relation to the sentence.
2. Usually at the start, followed by exclamation mark.
3. Can stand alone.

Common: Wow! Oh! Oops! Hey! Ah! Oh my god!

Example: Wow! This coffee is perfect.

 

## 句法

英语句法超通俗精讲（从零看懂所有句子）
先记核心：**英语句法 = 句子成分 + 五大基本句型 + 从句 + 语序规则**
所有英语句子，再长再复杂，都逃不开这套框架。

先搞懂 7大句子成分（必背）
句子就是不同成分拼起来的，一共7种：

1. **主语(S)**：谁/什么（动作发出者）
2. **谓语(V)**：做什么/是什么（动词核心，句子的心脏）
3. **宾语(O)**：动作承受者
4. **表语(P)**：跟在系动词后，说明主语**身份/状态**
5. **定语**：修饰**名词**（……的）
6. **状语**：修饰**动词/形容词/整句**（时间、地点、原因、方式、程度）
7. **补语**：补充说明主语/宾语，缺了句子意思不完整

口诀：**主谓宾、主系表，定状补**

 关键区分：谓语 & 系动词

- **实义动词**：有实际动作 → 吃、跑、看、做
- **系动词**：无动作，只起连接作用
  be(am/is/are) / 感官(look/sound/smell/taste/feel) / 变化(become/get)

英语五大基本句型（所有句子的母体）
所有简单句只有这5种，**长句都是简单句拼接出来的**

1. 主谓 S+V 主语 + 不及物动词（后面不能直接加宾语） - He **runs**. 他跑步。 - Birds **fly**. 鸟飞。
2. 主谓宾 S+V+O 主语 + 及物动词 + 宾语 - I **love** you. - She **reads** books.
3. 主系表 S+V+P 主语 + 系动词 + 表语 表语可以是名词/形容词/介词短语 - He **is** a teacher.（名词表语） - The flower **smells** nice.（形容词表语）
4. 主谓双宾 S+V+IO+DO 主语 + 动词 + **间接宾语(人)** + **直接宾语(物)** - He **gave** me a book. me=间接宾语，a book=直接宾语
5. 主谓宾宾补 S+V+O+C 主语+动词+宾语+宾语补足语（补充宾语） - We **make** him happy. him是宾语，happy补充说明him

 三、定语、状语怎么用（看懂长难句核心）
定语：修饰名词，翻译「……的」

- 位置分两种：
  - **前置定语**：单个形容词/名词 → a **beautiful** girl
  - **后置定语**：短语/从句放后面 the girl **under the tree** 树下的女孩

状语：修饰动作，时间/地点/原因/方式

- 位置很灵活，句首、句中、句尾都可以
- I study English **at home every day**. at home(地点) / every day(时间) 都是状语

 四、三大从句（英语句法难点）
简单句不够用，就用从句当**一个成分**

1. 名词性从句 整个从句当**名词**用：做主句、宾语、表语 - **宾语从句**：I know **that you are right**. - **主语从句**：**What he said** is true.
2. 定语从句（最常用） 从句当**定语**，修饰前面名词，也就是「……的人/物」 - The book **that I bought** is good. 我买的那本书很好
3. 状语从句 从句当**状语**，表时间、原因、条件、让步 - **When I was young**, I liked music.（时间） - I stay home **because it rains**.（原因）

 五、英语核心语序规则（和中文最大区别）
4. **主干语序**：和中文基本一样 **主谓宾**
5. **修饰语序**： - 中文：修饰词**放前面** - 英语：**短修饰在前，长修饰在后**
6. **疑问句**：助动词/be动词提前 You are happy → **Are** you happy?
7. **否定句**：助动词+not I like it → I **don't** like it.

 六、一句话总结句法逻辑
8. 先抓**主干**：先找主谓宾/主系表
9. 再拆**修饰**：定语找名词、状语找动词
10. 长句拆**从句**：把从句括号括起来，剩下就是简单句

### 动词

#### 时态

1. 一般时
   
   - **一般现在**：常态、习惯、真理 do / does I **get up** at 7.
   - **一般过去**：过去一次性动作 did I **went** home yesterday.
   - **一般将来**：将要做 will do / be going to do

2. 进行时（正在做） am/is/are **doing** was/were **doing**

3. 完成时（已经做完、影响现在） have/has **done** had **done**（过去的过去）
   
   1. 完成进行时（一直在做） have/has **been doing**
   
   记口诀
   **一般看时间，进行看正在，完成看影响**

#### 语态

- 主动：主语自己做 He cleans the room. 他打扫房间。

- 被动：主语**被**别人做 公式：**be + done** The room **is cleaned** by him. 房间被他打扫。
  
  时态套用被动
  
  - 一般现在被动：am/is/are done
  - 一般过去被动：was/were done
  - 将来被动：will be done
  - 现在完成被动：have/has been done

**记住：只要中文能翻出「被、受、遭」，必用被动**

#### 否定

用 not

1. 实义动词（有动作） 加 **don’t / doesn’t / didn’t** + 动词原形 - I like it → I **don’t like** it. - He likes it → He **doesn’t like** it.
2. be动词 / 情态动词(can/will/must) 直接后面加 **not** - He is here → He **isn’t** here. - I can do it → I **can’t** do it.

#### 虚拟语气

核心：**不是真的、假设、后悔、建议**

1. 与现在事实相反 If + 过去式，**would do**： If I **were** you, I **would study** hard. （我不是你，纯假设）
2. 与过去事实相反 If + had done，**would have done**： If I **had** studied, I **would have** passed. （当初没学，后悔）
3. wish
4. 建议/要求类 suggest / advise / demand 后： **(should) + 动词原形**： I suggest he **(should) go** at once.
   **口诀：现在用过去，过去用过完，建议原形不乱变**

### 倒装

- 部分倒装  Never **have I seen** him. 助动词 have 提前，实义动词 seen 在后
- 完全倒装 Here **comes** the bus. 整个谓语 comes 直接放主语 the bus 前面，**不用助动词**

正常：主谓宾
倒装：**助动词/be/情态 提到主语前**

1. 疑问句就是最基础倒装 You are happy → **Are** you happy?
2. 否定词放句首，必倒装 Never / Little / Hardly / Seldom / No sooner **Never have I seen** him. 我从没见过他。
3. Only + 状语放句首，倒装 **Only then did I realize** it.
4. So / Neither 倒装 - I like it. → **So do I.** 我也是。 - I don’t like it. → **Neither do I.** 我也不。
   **倒装本质：把助动词提前，后面动词变回原形**

### Non-Finite Verbs 非谓语动词

Form Key Function
to do purpose, intention, after certain verbs/adjectives
doing noun function (gerund), continuous action (participle)
done completed action, passive, perfect tenses
Clause subject + verb; independent or dependent

I'll explain the grammar of to do (infinitive), doing (gerund/present participle), and done (past participle), then cover clauses.

To do (Infinitive)

- Forms: to + base verb (to eat, to run)
- Use Example
- Purpose / intention I study hard to pass the exam.
- After certain adjectives I'm glad to see you.
- After certain verbs She wants to leave.
- As subject (formal) To err is human.
- Verbs commonly followed by infinitive: want, need, decide, hope, plan, promise, agree, refuse, learn, afford, fail, manage, tend, seem, appear
  Doing (Gerund / Present Participle)
- As Gerund (noun function):
- Use Example
- Subject Swimming is good exercise.
- Object I enjoy reading.
- After prepositions I'm good at singing.
- As Present Participle:
- Use Example
- Continuous tenses She is working now.
- Adjective The movie was boring.
- Verbs commonly followed by gerund: enjoy, finish, avoid, consider, suggest, mind, practice, quit, miss, imagine, deny, admit, postpone, risk, can't help, feel like, be worth, be used to, look forward to
  Done (Past Participle)
- Use Example
- Perfect tenses I have finished my work.
- Passive voice The cake was eaten.
- Adjective I'm done (finished). / The work is completed.
- Reduced relative clause The man injured in the accident was taken to hospital.

Key Differences: Infinitive vs. Gerund

- Some verbs change meaning:
- Verb + infinitive Verb + gerund
- I stopped to smoke (stopped in order to smoke) I stopped smoking (quit the habit)
- I remember to call (don't forget to do it) I remember calling (recall the memory)
- I forgot to lock (didn't do it) I forgot locking (don't recall doing it)
- I tried to open (attempted but difficult) I tried opening (experimented with a method)
- I regret to tell (sorry, I must say...) I regret telling (wish I hadn't said it)

### Clauses 主句、从句

Clauses

A clause is a group of words with a subject and a verb. There are two main types:

- Independent Clause (Main Clause) Can stand alone as a complete sentence. She sings beautifully. ✓ (complete sentence)
- Dependent Clause (Subordinate Clause)Cannot stand alone. Needs a main clause.

Type Function Example

- **Noun clause** acts as a subject/object: I know that she is busy.
- **Adjective clause** (relative clause) modifies a noun: The man who called is my boss.
- **Adverb clause** modifies verb/adj/adv: I left because I was tired.

Common Subordinating Conjunctions:

- Time: when, while, before, after, until, since, as soon as
- Reason: because, since, as
- Condition: if, unless, provided that
- Contrast: although, though, even though, while
- Purpose: so that, in order that

Examples in Sentences:

- When the rain stopped, we went outside. (adverb clause of time)
- The book that I borrowed is interesting. (adjective/relative clause)
- What he said surprised me. (noun clause as subject)

Relative Clauses (Who, Which, That, Whose, Whom) Type Example

- Defining (essential, no commas) The teacher who teaches math is nice.
- Non-defining (extra info, with commas) My brother, who lives in Paris, is visiting.

3 Most Important Clauses（考试+口语必用）

① Noun Clause 名词从句

- Works like a noun (thing / person / idea)
- Common start: that / if / whether / wh-words
- Examples:
  - I know that you are right.
  - I don’t know where he lives.
  - She asked if I was tired.

② Adjective Clause = Relative Clause 定语从句

- Modifies a noun
- Starts with: who / which / that / where / when / whose
- Examples:
  - The girl who is smiling is my friend.
  - The book that I bought is good.
  - This is the park where we met.

③ Adverbial Clause 状语从句

- Modifies verb / adjective / whole sentence
- Shows: time, reason, condition, purpose, contrast…
- Common linkers: when, because, if, although, until, so that
- Examples:
  - I will call you when I finish work. (time)
  - He stayed home because he was sick. (reason)
  - If you try hard, you will win. (condition)
  - Although it rained, we went out. (contrast)

## 简单语法

### 引言1

Here is a simple, practical grammar primer you can start using today.

Core idea

- English mostly uses Subject + Verb + Object (SVO) word order.
- Small words (a, the, in, on, not, is/are, do/does) carry a lot of meaning.

Parts of speech 词性

- Noun: a person, place, or thing (e.g., cat, city, idea)
- Pronoun: replaces a noun (I, you, he, she, it, they)
- Verb: action or state (eat, run, is, have)
- Adjective: describes a noun (red, big, interesting)
- Adverb: describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb (quickly, very)
- Determiner: articles and determiners (a, an, the, this, that)
- Preposition: shows a relationship (in, on, at, with)
- Conjunction: connects ideas (and, but, because)

Tenses 时态

- Simple present: I eat breakfast. You work every day.
- Present continuous: I am eating now. They are studying.
- Simple past: She walked to the store. We watched a movie.
- Simple future (will): He will help you. They will arrive soon.
- Be going to (planned future): I am going to visit mom.
  Tip: use simple present for routines, simple past for finished actions, present continuous for ongoing actions now.

Negatives

- Do/does not + base verb (present): I do not (don’t) like broccoli. She does not (doesn’t) bake often.
- Am/Is/Are not + present participle (for be-verbs + -ing): He is not coming today.

Questions

- Do/Does + subject + base verb: Do you like coffee? Does she sing?
- Be + subject (for questions with be): Are you ready? Is it raining?

Articles

- a/an = one, not specific. Use “a” before consonant sounds, “an” before vowel sounds.
- the = a specific item.

Plurals

- Plurals: usually add -s or -es (cats, boxes). Some irregulars (child -> children, man -> men).

Common quick fixes

- Subject-verb agreement: He walks, I walk.
- Irregular verbs: be (am/is/are), have (has), go (went, gone).
- Word order matters: “She reads a book” (not “Reads she a book”).

to do/doing/done

- to do: purpose, intention, after certain verbs/adjectives
- doing: noun function (gerund), continuous action (participle)
- done: completed action, passive, perfect tenses

### 引言2

8 Basic Parts of Speech (Foundation) Every English word belongs to one:
1. Noun → person / thing / place / idea: book, student, London, happiness
2. Pronoun → replace nouns: I, you, he, she, it, they, we, this, that
3. Verb → action / state: run, eat, study, is, are, have
4. Adjective → describe nouns: happy, tall, beautiful, cold
5. Adverb → describe verbs / adj / other adverbs: quickly, very, often, here, yesterday
6. Preposition → show time/place/relation: in, on, at, for, with, from, about
7. Conjunction → connect words/clauses: and, but, because, if, when, although
8. Article → a / an / the

5 Basic Sentence Structures. All English sentences follow these:
1. S + V (Subject + Verb): Birds fly.
2. S + V + O (Subject + Verb + Object): I love English.
3. S + V + C (Subject + Linking Verb + Complement): She is tired.
4. S + V + IO + DO (Indirect Object + Direct Object): He gave me a pen.
5. S + V + O + C: We keep the room clean.

All Important Verb Tenses (Most Used)

1. Simple **Present**: Habits, facts, daily life - I study English every day. - The sun rises in the east.
2. Present **Continuous**: Actions happening now - I am reading a book.
3. Simple **Past**: Finished actions in the past - I met my friend yesterday.
4. Past Continuous: Action in progress at a past time - I was sleeping at 10 PM last night.
5. Simple **Future**: Future plans - I will visit you tomorrow.
6. Present **Perfect**: Past action with present result - I have finished my homework.

English Grammar Made Simple

1. The Sentence
   A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.
   Subject: Who or what the sentence is about
   Verb: The action or state of being
   Example: She (subject) runs (verb).
2. Parts of Speech
   Nouns - Words that name people, places, things, or ideas.
   Examples: dog, city, happiness, John
   Pronouns - Words that replace nouns.
   Examples: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
   Verbs - Words that show action or state.
   Action: run, eat, write
   State: is, seem, become
   Adjectives - Words that describe nouns.
   Examples: happy, blue, tall, three
   Adverbs - Words that describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.
   Examples: quickly, very, always
   Prepositions - Words that show relationships (position, time, direction).
   Examples: in, on, at, to, from, with
   Conjunctions - Words that connect words or sentences.
   Examples: and, but, or, because
   Articles - Small words before nouns: a, an, the
3. Tenses (When things happen)
   Present: I eat - Use for now, habits
   Past: I ate - Use for completed actions
   Future: I will eat - Use for future actions
   Present Continuous: I am eating - Use for happening now
   Present Perfect: I have eaten - Use for past affecting present
4. Basic Sentence Structures
   Positive
   Subject + Verb: She sings.
   Subject + Verb + Object: She sings songs.
   Negative
   Add "not": She does not sing.
   Question
   Invert subject and verb: Does she sing?
5. Common Mistakes to Avoid
   Wrong: She don't like it
   Right: She doesn't like it
   Wrong: I have 20 years
   Right: I am 20 years old
   Wrong: He go to school
   Right: He goes to school
   Wrong: Me and John went
   Right: John and I went
6. Quick Tips
7. Subject-Verb Agreement: Singular subjects need singular verbs.
      He walks (not "He walk")
8. Use "a" before consonants, "an" before vowels:
      a cat, an apple
9. Adjectives before nouns:
      a beautiful day (not "a day beautiful")
10. Questions need helping verbs:
       Do you like it? (not "You like it?")
    Practice Makes Perfect!
    Start with simple sentences and gradually add more words. Read and listen to English every day!
